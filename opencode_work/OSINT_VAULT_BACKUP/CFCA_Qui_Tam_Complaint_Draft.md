## I. INTRODUCTION & NATURE OF ACTION
This is an action to recover treble damages and civil penalties under the Federal False Claims Act, 31 U.S.C. §§ 3729, and the California False Claims Act (CFCA), Cal. Gov. Code §§ 12650, on behalf of the United States and the State of California. 

Defendants engaged in a coordinated scheme to fraudulently misrepresent the environmental condition of two heavily contaminated parcels (17631 Cameron Lane and 17642 Beach Blvd) to secure **$6,094,847** in restricted Low and Moderate Income Housing Asset Fund (LMIHAF) capital and subsequent federal/state COVID-19 emergency operational grants. The site was built upon a known toxic baseline originally operated by Standard Oil (Tract 405) and contaminated with Hexavalent Chromium (Cr-VI) at 49 times the EPA limit. 

## II. THE FRAUDULENT SCHEME
### A. The "Use-It-Or-Lose-It" LMIHAF Deadline
In early 2020, the City of Huntington Beach faced state forfeiture of over $3,000,000 in excess LMIHAF surplus. To rapidly encumber the funds prior to the May 31, 2020 deadline, the City authorized the purchase of the target parcels from the Yamada Family Trusts, despite historical knowledge of unsealed agricultural wells and massive subterranean contamination.

### B. Falsification of Environmental Certifications
To execute the transaction using restricted housing funds, the City required an environmental clearance. 
1. **Defendant David Bernier (EEC Environmental)** fabricated a well-search narrative to obscure the existence of the 1947 Well W-4150, which acted as a direct conduit into the Talbert aquifer.
2. **Defendant Tamera Escobedo (OCHCA)** orchestrated the physical gerrymandering of the parcel addresses, consolidating Cameron Lane into Beach Blvd to illegally bypass California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) mandatory oversight.
3. **Defendant Anthony Martinez (OCHCA)** issued a fraudulent "Case Closed" certification (Case #20IC002) on August 21, 2020, actively concealing the Cr-VI plume and the 1955 Standard Oil (Permit C81252) infrastructure legacy.

### C. The Standard Oil / Tract 405 Concealment
Newly acquired municipal records from the Laserfiche WebLink portal (Folder ID 4806972) confirm that on October 25, 1955, Standard Oil Co. of California obtained Permit C81252 under the "Tract 405" designation for 17642 Beach Blvd. The Defendants actively suppressed this petrochemical baseline to expedite the LMIHAF funding.

### D. Ongoing Operational Fraud (Mercy House)
**Defendant Mercy House** currently operates the Huntington Beach Navigation Center (HBNC) atop the un-remediated Cr-VI plume. Mercy House continues to draw millions in state and federal continuum-of-care grants while failing to disclose the environmental endangerment of the 164+ displaced residents, constituting ongoing False Claims.

## III. CAUSES OF ACTION
**Count I: Presentation of False Claims (31 U.S.C. § 3729(a)(1)(A))**
Defendants knowingly presented false claims for payment (the LMIHAF disbursements and COVID-19 operational grants) based on fabricated environmental certifications.

**Count II: Making or Using False Records (31 U.S.C. § 3729(a)(1)(B))**
Defendants Escobedo, Martinez, and Bernier authored false records—including the Case #20IC002 certification and Phase I ESA—material to the fraudulent claims.
