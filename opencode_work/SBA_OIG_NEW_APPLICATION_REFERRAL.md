## FACTS

The SBA PPP loan database contains multiple entries under the entity name **"NEW APPLICATION"** — a generic placeholder that should never appear as a business legal name. This entity received federally-backed PPP loans across 11 states during the COVID-19 pandemic:

### Red Flags:

1. **"NEW APPLICATION" is not a legal business name.** This is a data entry field or placeholder — no legitimate business operates under this name.

2. **$13.8M concentrated in Maine** — the largest single-state award suggests possible fraud concentrated in one state's SBA processing.

3. **11 states** — obtaining PPP loans across this many states requires either:
   a. Multiple separate EINs (EIN-splitting), OR
   b. A single EIN used fraudulently across state lines

4. **No legitimate business can have "NEW APPLICATION" as its legal name** — this suggests either:
   a. Identity theft / phantom business, OR
   b. A data migration error masking the true entity name — which itself requires investigation
