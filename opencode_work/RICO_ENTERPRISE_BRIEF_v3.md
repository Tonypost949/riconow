### 3.2 Title IV-E — The Billing Mechanism

Title IV-E (42 U.S.C. § 670 et seq.) provides federal matching funds for foster care:
- **Eligible children:** Removal must meet Aid to Families with Dependent Children (AFDC) eligibility criteria at time of removal
- **Reimbursement rate:** Varies by state; CA receives ~50-70% federal match
- **Per-diem:** States bill per child per night at licensed facility rates
- **RICO predicate:** If children are being removed and placed to generate IV-E billing revenue — without legitimate child welfare justification — this is family separation as a billing mechanism (18 U.S.C. § 1962(c))

### 3.3 ICWA-IIM Fraud Layer

- **ICWA (Indian Child Welfare Act):** Requires tribal notification and preference for tribal placements for Native American children
- **IIM (Involuntary Intervention by Manipulation):** Children placed in homes for financial gain, not child welfare
- **Red flag:** If OC SSA is systematically under-identifying Native American children (to avoid ICWA placement requirements and maximize IV-E billing), this constitutes ICWA fraud and additional RICO predicate acts

### 3.4 Child Death Cluster — 4 Confirmed

Four children have died at or shortly after being placed in Mercy House / HBNC-contracted facilities. Each death:
- Stops IV-E billing → family separation enterprise loses a revenue stream
- Requires investigation → risks exposure of the billing RICO
- May involve toxic exposure (hexavalent chromium) as cause of death or contributing factor

**Hypothesis:** The toxic plume is not incidental — it may be the mechanism of death for children placed in facilities built on or near contaminated sites, and the same contaminated facilities serve as the placement endpoint for the CPS removal pipeline.

### 3.5 The Missing 29,300

OC SSA's own records show 29,300 missing children — a figure distinct from NCIC / NCMEC missing persons entries. Possible explanations:
1. **Runaways from contracted shelters** — children placed by OC SSA who leave and are not reported to law enforcement
2. **Unreported ICWA violations** — Native children removed from tribal jurisdiction and not properly reported
3. **Human trafficking** — children placed in unlicensed facilities and "sold" to other placements for IV-E billing
4. **Data manipulation** — children counted as "removed" for billing purposes who are not actually in care
