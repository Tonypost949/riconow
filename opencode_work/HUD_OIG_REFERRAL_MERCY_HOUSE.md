## REFERRAL SUMMARY

This referral concerns the potential misuse of federal pass-through grant funding in connection with Mercy House Living Centers, Inc. (EIN: 33-0912995), a nonprofit organization receiving significant federal HUD grants administered through the County of Orange and the City of Costa Mesa.

---

## FACTS SUPPORTING REFERRAL

### 1. Public Funds Used to Pay Private Lender — $6.5 Million
A federally-funded county grant appears to have been used to retire private debt owed to a CDFI lender, potentially constituting a prohibited use of federal grant funds.

**Details:**
- CM Mercy House CHDO LLC (affiliated entity) obtained a construction loan of **$7,900,000** from Century Housing Corporation (a CDFI/private lender)
- The loan was secured by a deed of trust on property rehabilitated using federal pass-through funding
- **$6,500,000 was paid down in February 2024 using County of Orange grant funds** — not Mercy House's own funds
- This means federal pass-through grant money was used to pay a private lender, raising questions about whether grant funds were used for their authorized purpose

**Basis for Concern:** Federal grant funds are subject to 2 CFR Part 200 Uniform Administrative Requirements. Using grant funds to pay down private debt without documented approval from the grantor agency may constitute an unallowable cost.

### 2. Related-Party Transactions Not Independently Reviewed
Per IRS Form 990 and GSA audit disclosures, Mercy House received $303,000 in FY2024 and $125,000 in FY2023 from Members of the Board of Directors. The independence of the board review process for these related-party transactions is unclear.

### 3. Deferred Debt Extraction Pattern
A $13,500,000 seller carryback note from the sale of the Vagabond Inn property (owned by Casa Aliento Mercy House CHDO LLC) has principal and interest deferred for 55 years — effectively creating a perpetual debt obligation that benefits the related LP structure while delaying any repayment accountability.

### 4. Questioned Costs — Grant Compliance
The GSA audit (Weaver & Tidwell, FY2024) identified findings related to internal controls over financial reporting and compliance with Uniform Guidance. Specific questioned costs have been noted in the Schedule of Findings and Questioned Costs (pp. 39-40 of audit report).

---

## REQUESTED ACTION

1. **Investigate** whether the $6.5M County of Orange grant payment to Century Housing Corporation was an authorized use of federal pass-through funds under the applicable grant agreement(s)

2. **Review** HUD OIG's oversight of CHDO program funds flowing through County of Orange to Mercy House CHDO entities

3. **Examine** whether the SBA PPP loan ($1,339,000, forgiven 2021) was properly used for payroll and not diverted to other uses

4. **Audit** HUD grants to Mercy House for compliance with 2 CFR Part 200, including subrecipient monitoring requirements
