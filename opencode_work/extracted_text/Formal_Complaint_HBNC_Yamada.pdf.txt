FORMAL COMPLAINT & NOTICE OF VIOLATION
Civil Rights, Funding Diversion, and Historic Mismanagement
TO: CALIFORNIA STATE AUDITOR; CA DEPT. OF HOUSING (HCD); OC GRAND JURY
SUBJECT: ILLEGAL DETENTION, MISUSE OF LMIHAF FUNDS, AND SB 535 DAC VIOLATIONS AT
17642 BEACH BLVD / 17631 CAMERON LN, HUNTINGTON BEACH
1. ILLEGAL DETENTION & FREEDOM OF MOVEMENT
The Huntington Beach Navigation Center (HBNC) operates under a 'Good Neighbor Policy' that prohibits
'walk-outs' and 'walk-ups.' This constitutes a restriction of movement for U.S. citizens who are not under
judicial warrant or medical hold. This 'Color of Law' violation effectively creates a gated detention facility on
the site of the historic Yamada property - land formerly associated with Japanese-American internment. This
is a profound mark of disrespect to our Sister City, Anjo, and the survivors of the 1940s camps.
2. MISUSE OF RESTRICTED HOUSING FUNDS (LMIHAF)
The City of Huntington Beach utilized $6.1 million from the Low-Moderate Income Housing Asset Fund
(LMIHAF) to purchase this land. Under Health & Safety Code 34176.1, these funds are strictly for
PERMANENT affordable housing. The current 'temporary' Sprung structure has been used to hold citizens
for years, violating the legal mandate for permanent housing and bypassing habitability standards.
3. DISADVANTAGED COMMUNITY (DAC) & GREEN FUND DIVERSION
This site is a designated SB 535 Disadvantaged Community (DAC). These census tracts are legally
prioritized for Green Projects and environmental remediation. Instead of remediation for a neighborhood
historically polluted by oil interests, the City has installed a high-impact industrial tent with no green
infrastructure, air filtration, or permanent community benefit.
4. MEDICAL FACILITY FRAUD
The facility purports to offer 'medical navigation' and health services. However, it denies public access. Under
HSC 1200, a clinic must serve the public. Accepting state medical funds (CalAIM/Medi-Cal) while enforcing
an exclusionary Good Neighbor Policy is regulatory fraud.
DEMAND:
Immediate audit of the $6.1M LMIHAF expenditure, investigation into the restriction of movement without
judicial oversight, and a full accounting of SB 535 Green Project funds for this census tract.
