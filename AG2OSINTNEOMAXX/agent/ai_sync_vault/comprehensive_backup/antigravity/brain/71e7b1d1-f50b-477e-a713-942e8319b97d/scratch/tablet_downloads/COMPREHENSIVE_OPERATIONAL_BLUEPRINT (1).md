## PART 1: LEGAL FRAMEWORK & FOUNDATIONAL MECHANICS

### 1.1 Core Legal Doctrines

#### A. Sham Nonprofit / Alter Ego Doctrine
**Triggered when:** Nonprofit exists ONLY to benefit corporation (no independent charitable purpose)

**Outcome:**
- Tax-exempt status revoked retroactively
- All prior income taxable back to incorporation
- Officers face personal liability
- Corporate veil pierced immediately (not discretionary)
- Assets frozen same day as preliminary injunction
- Criminal referral (IRS + FBI parallel track)

**Speed:** Preliminary injunction in 2-3 weeks; veil piercing ruling in 60-90 days

---

#### B. Captive/Subsidiary Foundation Doctrine
**Triggered when:** Nonprofit does SOME real charitable work BUT disproportionately benefits parent corporation

**Profile indicators:**
- 501(c)(3) private foundation, substantially/entirely funded by one corporate parent
- Grant distributions = tiny fraction of parent revenue (e.g., $1M charity from $1B corporation)
- Board composition = parent employees (control, not independence)
- Grant recipients = strategically aligned with parent's business interests, not randomly charitable
- Geographically concentrated around parent's operating locations
- Commingling of assets/decisions (nonprofit doesn't act independently)

**Outcome:**
- Private benefit doctrine violation (IRC § 501(c)(3))
- Self-dealing violation (IRC § 4941)
- Lobbying substitution violation (IRC § 4945)
- Veil piercing based on subsidiary structure (not fraud intent)
- Asset freezes without waiting for final judgment
- Automatic criminal referral (tax fraud prosecutions)

**Speed:** Preliminary injunction in 2-3 weeks; summary judgment veil piercing in 60-90 days (because structure is documentary, not disputed)

---

#### C. Lobbying Substitution Rule (IRC § 4945)
**Triggered when:** Foundation grants used to neutralize local political opposition or buy political goodwill

**What it catches:**
- Grants to civic organizations where parent has business interests
- Scholarships to children of government officials
- Funding of police/athletic leagues (benefits officers whose departments contract with parent)
- Funding of local charities where board members are parent's lobbyists

**Outcome:**
- Tax penalty ($5,000-$100,000 per violation)
- Excise tax on foundation
- Loss of tax exemption
- Criminal prosecution for officers
- Evidence of intentional structure (not accident)

---

#### D. False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. § 3729)
**Triggered when:** Nonprofit receives government funding while providing false/misleading information about corporate ownership, use of funds, or charitable purpose

**Why it matters:**
- Federal law (NO preemption defense possible)
- Applies even if fraud is indirect/subtle
- Qui tam provision: private citizen can sue on behalf of government
- Treble damages (3x recovery) + penalties
- Applies to environmental contamination cases (false statements about safety)

**Speed:** Qui tam complaint filed = federal complaint filed = investigation begins immediately

---

#### E. RICO (18 U.S.C. § 1962)
**Triggered when:** Parent + nonprofit form "pattern of racketeering activity" (2+ predicate offenses over 10-year period)

**What counts as predicate offenses:**
- Wire fraud (misrepresenting charitable purpose)
- Money laundering (funneling corporate money through nonprofit)
- Tax fraud (false 990 filings)
- Conspiracy to commit any of the above

**Outcome:**
- Criminal penalties: 20 years per count
- Civil RICO: treble damages + attorney fees
- Asset forfeiture (everything involved in the scheme)
- No statute of limitations on RICO conspiracy
- RICO conspiracy charge doesn't require proof predicate offenses occurred (just that agreement to commit them existed)

**Speed:** RICO count adds to federal indictment = parallel criminal/civil tracks

### 4.2 Why Their Preemption Defense FAILS

**Federal Supremacy Clause only works when:**
- State law **conflicts** with federal law
- State law **prevents** performance of federal duty
- Federal law **explicitly preempts** state law

**But federal supremacy does NOT shield against:**

#### 1. False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. § 3729)
- **Federal law** (preemption defense impossible)
- Applies to state AND federal crimes
- Qui tam provision: private citizen sues on behalf of government
- No state authority can be preempted from enforcing a FEDERAL crime
- Corporation cannot claim state AG is violating federal law by prosecuting federal crimes

#### 2. RICO (18 U.S.C. § 1962)
- **Federal law** (preemption defense impossible)
- Applies to pattern of state AND federal crimes
- Cannot be preempted because it IS federal law
- If corporation claims preemption = admits federal RICO violation exists

#### 3. Elder Fraud (State AND Federal Statutes)
- State elder fraud laws do NOT conflict with federal law
- Federal law actually enhances state prosecution (SSA OIG + FBI involvement)
- Cannot be preempted

#### 4. Environmental Violations (Clean Water Act + State Environmental Law)
- Federal statute is a floor, not a ceiling
- States can enforce stricter environmental standards
- No preemption doctrine applies
- If nonprofit caused environmental contamination = state CAN prosecute under both state + federal law

#### 5. Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1343)
- Federal crime, but state can prosecute under state wire fraud statutes too
- No conflict, no preemption
