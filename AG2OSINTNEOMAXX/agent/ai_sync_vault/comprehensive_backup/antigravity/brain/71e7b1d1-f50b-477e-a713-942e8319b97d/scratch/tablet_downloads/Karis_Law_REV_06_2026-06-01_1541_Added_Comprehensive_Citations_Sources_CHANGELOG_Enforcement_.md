## PART I: THE LEGAL FOUNDATION

### The Core Argument

The FCC designated N11 codes (211, 311, 411, 511, 611, 711, 811, 911) as regulated communication services. AI now performs identical functions on communication devices. Under existing law, AI services are subject to the same regulatory framework.

**Key Precedent:** When cell phones emerged, regulators adapted existing telecom law to cover the new technology. When the internet emerged, the Telecommunications Act of 1996 was passed within ~6 years. AI (ChatGPT launched November 2022) has operated in a regulatory enforcement gap for 3.5+ years — not because the law doesn't exist, but because it isn't being enforced.

---

### The Statutes That Already Apply

#### 1. Communications Act of 1934 — Title II Common Carrier Obligations

**47 U.S.C. § 201 — Service and Charges**
> *"(a) It shall be the duty of every common carrier engaged in interstate or foreign communication by wire or radio to furnish such communication service upon reasonable request..."*
>
> *"(b) All charges, practices, classifications, and regulations for and in connection with such communication service, shall be just and reasonable, and any such charge, practice, classification, or regulation that is unjust or unreasonable is declared to be unlawful."*

**Application to AI:** Any AI service functioning as a communication intermediary (information retrieval, directory services, social service referrals) must operate under just and reasonable practices. Hallucinated information, biased outputs, and denial of service are unlawful under this statute.

---

#### 2. 47 U.S.C. § 202 — Discrimination and Preferences

> *"(a) It shall be unlawful for any common carrier to make any unjust or unreasonable discrimination in charges, practices, classifications, regulations, facilities, or services... or to make or give any undue or unreasonable preference or advantage to any particular person, class of persons, or locality."*

**Application to AI:** AI services cannot discriminate in service delivery based on race, class, location, or economic status. Algorithmic bias that produces discriminatory outcomes violates this statute.

---

#### 3. 47 U.S.C. § 222 — Privacy of Customer Information

> *"(a) Every telecommunications carrier has a duty to protect the confidentiality of proprietary information of, and relating to... customers."*

**Application to AI:** Every AI service operating as a communication tool has an obligation to protect user data. Data harvesting, selling user information, and breaches violate this existing statute. AT&T was fined $177 million; T-Mobile $31.5 million under this authority. AI companies face zero enforcement.

---

#### 4. 47 U.S.C. § 230 — Communications Decency Act (Section 230)

> *"(c)(1) No provider or user of an interactive computer service shall be treated as the publisher or speaker of any information provided by another information content provider."*

**Application to AI:** AI companies currently hide behind Section 230 immunity. However, AI-generated content is NOT third-party content — it is produced by the service itself. Section 230 was never intended to shield companies from liability for their own generated outputs.

---

#### 5. 47 U.S.C. § 623 — Kari's Law (Direct 911 Access)

> *"A person engaged in the business of manufacturing, importing, selling, or leasing multi-line telephone systems may not manufacture, import, sell, or lease a multi-line telephone system, unless such system is pre-configured such that, when properly installed... a user may directly initiate a call to 9-1-1 without dialing any additional digit, code, prefix, or post-fix."*

**THE CORNERSTONE PRECEDENT:** If a communication device is CAPABLE of connecting to emergency services, you CANNOT block that capability. AI on phones and devices is capable of emergency communication. Under Kari's Law principle, blocking AI from emergency service access on a communication device is unlawful.

**Background:** Kari Hunt was murdered in a hotel room in 2013. Her 9-year-old daughter attempted to call 911 four times but could not reach emergency services because the hotel phone system required dialing "9" for an outside line. This law ensures no communication barrier stands between a person in danger and emergency help.

---

#### 6. RAY BAUM's Act — Section 506 (Dispatchable Location)

> *Requires that "dispatchable location" information be conveyed with 911 calls so that first responders can locate the caller.*

**Application to AI:** AI devices with GPS, WiFi, and network triangulation capabilities must convey accurate location data during emergency communications. The technology exists. Failing to implement it is a choice — and under RAY BAUM's Act, an unlawful one.

---

#### 7. 15 U.S.C. § 45 — FTC Act, Section 5 (Unfair or Deceptive Practices)

> *"(a)(1) Unfair methods of competition in or affecting commerce, and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in or affecting commerce, are hereby declared unlawful."*

**Application to AI:** The FTC has explicitly stated: *"There is no AI exemption from the laws on the books."* (FTC Press Release, September 2024). AI services making deceptive claims about accuracy, privacy, or capability are already violating Section 5. The American Bar Association published analysis in 2026 confirming the FTC is actively enforcing Section 5(a) in AI markets.

---

#### 8. Americans with Disabilities Act — Title IV (Telecommunications)

> *Requires telecommunications companies to provide a nationwide system of telecommunications relay services (TRS) to enable people with hearing or speech disabilities to communicate.*

**Application to AI:** The FCC mandated 711 dialing for nationwide TRS access. AI services must be equally accessible to disabled users. AI that cannot serve deaf, blind, or speech-impaired users on communication devices violates ADA Title IV.

---

#### 9. 18 U.S.C. § 1030 — Computer Fraud and Abuse Act

> *Prohibits unauthorized access to protected computers, computer fraud, and related cyber offenses.*

**Application to AI/Cyber Emergency:** When data breaches occur, existing criminal law applies. The absence of a real-time emergency response system for cybercrime does not mean the law doesn't exist — it means enforcement infrastructure hasn't been built.

---

#### 10. Pipeline Safety Improvement Act / 49 U.S.C. — (811 Authority)

> *Federal mandate requiring notification before excavation to prevent damage to underground utilities.*

**Application to AI:** The principle of mandatory safety notification before potentially dangerous activity applies equally to digital infrastructure. AI systems interacting with critical infrastructure must have safety protocols.

---

#### 11. Child Abuse Prevention and Treatment Act (CAPTA) — 42 U.S.C. § 5106a

> *Requires states to establish mandatory reporting provisions for suspected child abuse and neglect as a condition of receiving federal funding.*

**Application to AI:** CAPTA established the federal framework that requires every state to maintain mandatory reporting laws. Mandatory reporters (doctors, lawyers, teachers, social workers, nurses, counselors, law enforcement, clergy) are **legally obligated** to report suspected abuse the moment they become aware of it. Failure to report is a **criminal misdemeanor** in most states, punishable by up to 6 months in jail and/or $1,000 fine. In civil court, mandatory reporters can be held **liable for all damages** resulting from failure to report.

**The 999 Application:** If AI can identify mandatory reporters in the vicinity of an emergency and notify them of suspected abuse, those individuals are NOW AWARE — and their legal obligation to report is triggered. The AI doesn't replace mandatory reporting. It activates it.

---

#### 12. Elder Justice Act — 42 U.S.C. § 1397j

> *Establishes mandatory reporting requirements for suspected elder abuse in federally funded facilities.*

**Application to AI:** Extends the mandatory reporting framework to vulnerable elderly populations. AI services interacting with or aware of elder abuse have the same obligation to route to appropriate authorities.
