# KARI'S LAW FOR AI: The Triple-Digit Emergency & Service Framework

## A Legal Framework for AI Communication Services Under Existing Law

**Authored by:** Anthony DiMarcello  
**Date:** June 1, 2026

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

AI is a communication device. It operates on communication devices. It performs the same functions as regulated communication services. Existing federal law already covers it — regulators have simply failed to enforce.

This framework bootstraps every existing N11 service code with an AI-enhanced triple-digit (NNN) equivalent. **Nothing is replaced. Everything is upgraded.**

The legal authority already exists. This document maps every service to the statutes that mandate its regulation.

## PART I: THE LEGAL FOUNDATION

### The Core Argument

The FCC designated N11 codes (211, 311, 411, 511, 611, 711, 811, 911) as regulated communication services. AI now performs identical functions on communication devices. Under existing law, AI services are subject to the same regulatory framework.

**Key Precedent:** When cell phones emerged, regulators adapted existing telecom law to cover the new technology. When the internet emerged, the Telecommunications Act of 1996 was passed within ~6 years. AI (ChatGPT launched November 2022) has operated in a regulatory enforcement gap for 3.5+ years — not because the law doesn't exist, but because it isn't being enforced.

---

### The Statutes That Already Apply

#### 1. Communications Act of 1934 — Title II Common Carrier Obligations

**47 U.S.C. § 201 — Service and Charges**
> *"(a) It shall be the duty of every common carrier engaged in interstate or foreign communication by wire or radio to furnish such communication service upon reasonable request..."*
>
> *"(b) All charges, practices, classifications, and regulations for and in connection with such communication service, shall be just and reasonable, and any such charge, practice, classification, or regulation that is unjust or unreasonable is declared to be unlawful."*

**Application to AI:** Any AI service functioning as a communication intermediary (information retrieval, directory services, social service referrals) must operate under just and reasonable practices. Hallucinated information, biased outputs, and denial of service are unlawful under this statute.

---

#### 2. 47 U.S.C. § 202 — Discrimination and Preferences

> *"(a) It shall be unlawful for any common carrier to make any unjust or unreasonable discrimination in charges, practices, classifications, regulations, facilities, or services... or to make or give any undue or unreasonable preference or advantage to any particular person, class of persons, or locality."*

**Application to AI:** AI services cannot discriminate in service delivery based on race, class, location, or economic status. Algorithmic bias that produces discriminatory outcomes violates this statute.

---

#### 3. 47 U.S.C. § 222 — Privacy of Customer Information

> *"(a) Every telecommunications carrier has a duty to protect the confidentiality of proprietary information of, and relating to... customers."*

**Application to AI:** Every AI service operating as a communication tool has an obligation to protect user data. Data harvesting, selling user information, and breaches violate this existing statute. AT&T was fined $177 million; T-Mobile $31.5 million under this authority. AI companies face zero enforcement.

---

#### 4. 47 U.S.C. § 230 — Communications Decency Act (Section 230)

> *"(c)(1) No provider or user of an interactive computer service shall be treated as the publisher or speaker of any information provided by another information content provider."*

**Application to AI:** AI companies currently hide behind Section 230 immunity. However, AI-generated content is NOT third-party content — it is produced by the service itself. Section 230 was never intended to shield companies from liability for their own generated outputs.

---

#### 5. 47 U.S.C. § 623 — Kari's Law (Direct 911 Access)

> *"A person engaged in the business of manufacturing, importing, selling, or leasing multi-line telephone systems may not manufacture, import, sell, or lease a multi-line telephone system, unless such system is pre-configured such that, when properly installed... a user may directly initiate a call to 9-1-1 without dialing any additional digit, code, prefix, or post-fix."*

**THE CORNERSTONE PRECEDENT:** If a communication device is CAPABLE of connecting to emergency services, you CANNOT block that capability. AI on phones and devices is capable of emergency communication. Under Kari's Law principle, blocking AI from emergency service access on a communication device is unlawful.

**Background:** Kari Hunt was murdered in a hotel room in 2013. Her 9-year-old daughter attempted to call 911 four times but could not reach emergency services because the hotel phone system required dialing "9" for an outside line. This law ensures no communication barrier stands between a person in danger and emergency help.

---

#### 6. RAY BAUM's Act — Section 506 (Dispatchable Location)

> *Requires that "dispatchable location" information be conveyed with 911 calls so that first responders can locate the caller.*

**Application to AI:** AI devices with GPS, WiFi, and network triangulation capabilities must convey accurate location data during emergency communications. The technology exists. Failing to implement it is a choice — and under RAY BAUM's Act, an unlawful one.

---

#### 7. 15 U.S.C. § 45 — FTC Act, Section 5 (Unfair or Deceptive Practices)

> *"(a)(1) Unfair methods of competition in or affecting commerce, and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in or affecting commerce, are hereby declared unlawful."*

**Application to AI:** The FTC has explicitly stated: *"There is no AI exemption from the laws on the books."* (FTC Press Release, September 2024). AI services making deceptive claims about accuracy, privacy, or capability are already violating Section 5. The American Bar Association published analysis in 2026 confirming the FTC is actively enforcing Section 5(a) in AI markets.

---

#### 8. Americans with Disabilities Act — Title IV (Telecommunications)

> *Requires telecommunications companies to provide a nationwide system of telecommunications relay services (TRS) to enable people with hearing or speech disabilities to communicate.*

**Application to AI:** The FCC mandated 711 dialing for nationwide TRS access. AI services must be equally accessible to disabled users. AI that cannot serve deaf, blind, or speech-impaired users on communication devices violates ADA Title IV.

---

#### 9. 18 U.S.C. § 1030 — Computer Fraud and Abuse Act

> *Prohibits unauthorized access to protected computers, computer fraud, and related cyber offenses.*

**Application to AI/Cyber Emergency:** When data breaches occur, existing criminal law applies. The absence of a real-time emergency response system for cybercrime does not mean the law doesn't exist — it means enforcement infrastructure hasn't been built.

---

#### 10. Pipeline Safety Improvement Act / 49 U.S.C. — (811 Authority)

> *Federal mandate requiring notification before excavation to prevent damage to underground utilities.*

**Application to AI:** The principle of mandatory safety notification before potentially dangerous activity applies equally to digital infrastructure. AI systems interacting with critical infrastructure must have safety protocols.

## PART II: THE TRIPLE-DIGIT FRAMEWORK

**Principle:** Every existing N11 service is bootstrapped — not replaced — with an AI-enhanced NNN equivalent. The original service continues to operate. The AI layer enhances, accelerates, and extends it.

---

### 222 — AI Social Services (Bootstraps 211)

**What 211 Does:** Free, confidential referrals for food, housing, healthcare, mental health, disaster relief, veteran services.

**What 222 Adds:**
- 24/7 AI-powered instant social service matching — no hold times, no business hours
- Real-time eligibility screening across all available programs simultaneously
- Multi-language support with cultural competency
- Proactive outreach when AI detects a user may need services
- Warm handoff to live 211 specialists when needed
- Privacy protections under §222 — user data is NEVER harvested or sold

**Governing Statutes:**
- 47 U.S.C. § 201 (just and reasonable service)
- 47 U.S.C. § 202 (no discrimination — services must reach all communities equally)
- 47 U.S.C. § 222 (confidentiality of user information)
- 15 U.S.C. § 45 (no deceptive practices — AI cannot hallucinate resources that don't exist)

---

### 333 — AI Municipal Government Services (Bootstraps 311)

**What 311 Does:** Non-emergency municipal services — report potholes, noise complaints, city information, public records.

**What 333 Adds:**
- Instant AI-powered government service routing
- Automated form filing and complaint submission
- Real-time status tracking on service requests
- Public records search and accessibility
- Accountability tracking — AI monitors response times and flags failures
- Transparent data on how tax dollars are being used

**Governing Statutes:**
- 47 U.S.C. § 201 (duty to furnish service upon reasonable request)
- 47 U.S.C. § 202 (no discrimination in service delivery by locality)
- Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. § 552) — AI accelerates public records access

---

### 444 — AI Information & Directory Services (Bootstraps 411)

**What 411 Does:** Phone number and address lookup for businesses and individuals.

**What 444 Adds:**
- AI-powered universal information service
- Business lookup, verification, and reviews
- Scam and fraud detection on businesses and phone numbers
- Verified information with source attribution — no hallucinations
- Accessibility for all users regardless of technical ability

**Governing Statutes:**
- 47 U.S.C. § 201 (common carrier obligation to provide information services)
- 47 U.S.C. § 202 (equal access regardless of user demographics)
- 15 U.S.C. § 45 (information must be accurate, not deceptive)

**Key Precedent:** 411 operators were subject to common carrier regulation. AI performing the identical function must be subject to identical regulation. The medium changed. The obligation did not.

### 555 — AI Transportation & Traffic Services (Bootstraps 511)

**What 511 Does:** Real-time traffic conditions, road closures, transit information.

**What 555 Adds:**

### 777 — AI Accessibility & Relay Services (Bootstraps 711)

**What 711 Does:** Telecommunications relay services for deaf, hard of hearing, and speech-impaired individuals.

**What 777 Adds:**

**Key Precedent:** The FCC mandated 711 for nationwide relay access in 2000. AI accessibility services are the modern equivalent and must be held to the same standard.

### 888 — AI Infrastructure & Cyber Safety (Bootstraps 811)

**What 811 Does:** "Call Before You Dig" — locate underground utilities before excavation.

**What 888 Adds:**

- Immediate credit freeze across all three bureaus

**Governing Statutes:**

**The Gap This Fills:** There is currently NO real-time emergency response for cybercrime. At 3AM when your identity is being stolen, your options are: fill out a web form. 888 changes that.

### 999 — AI Emergency Response (Bootstraps 911)

**What 911 Does:** Emergency dispatch for police, fire, EMS.

**What 999 Does — The Full Protocol:**

#### Phase 1: The Call
- User dials **999** on any device
- AI immediately routes a call to 911
- AI generates a looping voice message to the 911 operator:
  > *"A person is in danger and needs emergency assistance. Location: [GPS coordinates/street address]. This is an automated emergency relay. The caller cannot speak safely. Repeating..."*
- No voice required from the victim

#### Phase 2: Stealth Mode
- Device screen goes **completely dark** — appears powered off
- No sounds, no vibrations, no visual indicators
- Abuser/attacker has NO indication a call was made
- Victim is not punished for seeking help

#### Phase 3: Silent Witness
- Device begins recording all ambient audio
- Everything is timestamped and cryptographically preserved
- Evidence is uploaded to secure cloud storage in real-time
- Creates admissible evidence for prosecution
- The device becomes the witness when no human witness exists

#### Phase 4: OSINT Intelligence Gathering
- AI pulls all available data about the environment:
  - GPS/cell tower triangulation for precise location
  - Connected WiFi networks (identifies the building/location)
  - Nearby Bluetooth devices (identifies people/devices present)
  - IP addresses and network information
  - Any metadata that establishes the scene
- Builds a real-time intelligence profile for first responders

#### Phase 5: Environmental Activation
- AI reaches out to every connected device in range:
  - **Other phones nearby** → attempts to trigger 911 calls
  - **Smart speakers** → announces: *"Emergency services have been contacted. You are being recorded."*
  - **Smart lights** → flash to signal distress / guide first responders
  - **Connected cameras** → begin recording
  - **Any IoT device** → activated as deterrent or witness
- The abuser's own technology turns against them

**Who This Protects:**
- Domestic violence victims who cannot speak
- Children being abused
- Trafficking victims
- Elderly being exploited
- Disabled individuals who cannot use traditional 911
- Anyone with no power to say no

**Governing Statutes:**
- **47 U.S.C. § 623 — Kari's Law** (THE cornerstone: if a communication device CAN connect to emergency services, you CANNOT block that capability)
- **RAY BAUM's Act, Section 506** (dispatchable location MUST be conveyed — AI devices have GPS, WiFi, and network data that exceeds traditional phone location accuracy)
- 47 U.S.C. § 201 (duty to furnish communication service — emergency service is the most fundamental duty)
- 47 U.S.C. § 222 (privacy protections for the victim's data)
- ADA Title IV (accessible emergency communication for disabled users)

**Key Precedent:** Kari Hunt died because a phone system placed a barrier between her daughter and 911. AI companies that fail to implement emergency access on communication-capable devices are creating the same barrier. The law already prohibits this.
