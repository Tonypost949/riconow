**Court's Analysis and Rulings:**

#### I. STANDING FOR RCRA INJUNCTIVE RELIEF — **DISMISSED**
- Court found Plaintiff **LACKS STANDING** to seek injunctive relief under RCRA
- **Reasoning:** Plaintiff stopped living at shelter in October 2024; therefore no longer facing imminent threat
- Court cited: *Express Car Wash Corp. v. Irinaga Bros., Inc.*, 967 F. Supp. 1188 (D. Or. 1997) — requires "imminent threat from hazardous waste"
- Case law: Plaintiff who is no longer "proximate" to contaminated property lacks standing
- Court found Plaintiff cannot show alleged injuries can be "redressed by a favorable RCRA decision"

#### II. STANDING FOR TITLE VI DECLARATORY RELIEF — **DISMISSED**
- Court found Plaintiff **LACKS STANDING** for Title VI claim
- **Problem 1 — Past Harm Doctrine:** "Neither injunctive nor declaratory relief may be premised on past harm" (*Jiminez v. Service Employees Int'l Union Local 775*, 590 F. Supp. 3d 1349 (E.D. Wash. 2022))
- **Problem 2 — Personal Discrimination:** Plaintiff must allege **PERSONAL discrimination** on basis of race, color, or national origin per Title VI requirements
  - Plaintiff cannot assert Title VI claim on behalf of others (*Fobbs v. Holy Cross Health System Corp.*, 29 F.3d 1439 (9th Cir. 1994))
- **Problem 3 — Disability Not Covered:** Title VI only covers race, color, or national origin discrimination
  - Does NOT cover disability discrimination (*Foster v. Michigan*, 573 F. App'x 377 (6th Cir. 2014); *Price v. Texas Tech Univ.*, 784 F. Supp. 3d 944 (W.D. Tex. 2025))

#### III. RES JUDICATA BAR — **FLAGGED AS POSSIBLE DEFENSE**
- Court noted Plaintiff already received **$6,000 from settlement** with one of the Defendants
- Raises potential **RES JUDICATA** (claim preclusion) issue
- Suggests prior litigation may have already resolved these allegations

### 1. **Standing Deficiency (Core Problem)**
- Plaintiff no longer resides at shelter (departed October 2024)
- Court found "imminent threat" requirement of RCRA cannot be satisfied
- Past harm cannot support injunctive or declaratory relief under current law

### 2. **Title VI Claim Deficiencies**
- Disability claims not covered by Title VI (race/color/national origin only)
- Requires personal discrimination allegation, not group-based claims
